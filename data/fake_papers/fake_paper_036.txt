### 1. Abstract
Semi-supervised learning (SSL) remains a central challenge in machine learning, particularly when labeled data is scarce. While consistency regularization has emerged as a dominant paradigm, existing methods often rely on simple pointwise perturbations, failing to capture the underlying manifold structure of the data. In this paper, we propose Stochastic Latent-Graph Consistency (SLGC), a novel framework that integrates topological regularization with stochastic augmentation. By constructing dynamic graphs in the latent space and enforcing consistency across graph-perturbed augmentations, SLGC compels the model to respect the manifold geometry during label propagation. We formulate this as an optimization problem over a stochastic graph Laplacian, providing a theoretical bound on the generalization error. Experiments on CIFAR-10, SVHN, and mini-ImageNet demonstrate that SLGC consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, providing a robust mechanism for leveraging unlabeled data in low-label regimes.

**Keywords:** Semi-Supervised Learning, Consistency Regularization, Stochastic Graphs, Manifold Learning, Topological Regularization.

### 2. Introduction
The disparity between the abundance of unlabeled data and the scarcity of curated labels has spurred significant interest in semi-supervised learning (SSL) [1]. Standard empirical risk minimization (ERM) is insufficient for unlabeled data, necessitating the use of inductive biases such as the smoothness assumption [2]. Recent advancements in consistency regularization, exemplified by methods like FixMatch and MixMatch, enforce output stability under stochastic perturbations [3, 4]. However, these approaches treat samples as independent entities, neglecting the global geometric structure of the data manifold.

Existing consistency-based SSL methods typically employ data-level augmentation (e.g., flipping, rotation) [5]. While effective for local robustness, these methods are often limited by the quality of the augmentation policy and the local nature of the consistency constraint [6]. If the augmentation is insufficient, the model may collapse into a degenerate solution, failing to propagate information across sparse labeled regions. This work addresses this limitation by introducing the Stochastic Latent-Graph Consistency (SLGC) framework. 

SLGC leverages the hypothesis that the decision boundary should align with the low-density regions of the data manifold [7]. By constructing a stochastic graph in the latent space, SLGC enforces consistency not just between two views of the same sample, but across the entire graph neighborhood. This approach mitigates the sensitivity to augmentation noise and promotes the formation of tightly clustered latent representations for intra-class samples [8]. Theoretical analysis provided in this paper suggests that the SLGC objective minimizes the spectral norm of the Laplacian, effectively regularizing the manifold learning process [9].

The contributions of this paper are three-fold: 
1. We introduce the SLGC framework, a novel architecture that integrates topological consistency into standard SSL training loops.
2. We provide a mathematical formulation for stochastic graph-based regularization, demonstrating its connection to the Dirichlet energy of the manifold.
3. We conduct extensive empirical evaluations on standard benchmarks, showing that SLGC yields substantial performance gains over existing consistency-regularization techniques.

---

### 3. Related Work
SSL has evolved from traditional transductive graph methods [10] to modern deep generative models [11]. The primary development in recent years has been the refinement of consistency regularization [3, 12]. Methods such as $\Pi$-model and Mean Teacher established the utility of temporal ensembles in stabilizing target predictions [13, 14]. More recent developments, such as UDA and ReMixMatch, have incorporated advanced augmentation strategies to enhance the diversity of the consistency constraints [15, 16].

Topological and graph-based approaches have long been associated with SSL, dating back to spectral clustering and manifold regularizers [17, 18]. However, traditional graph methods suffered from poor scalability. Recent efforts to integrate graphs into deep learning, such as Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs) and their variants, have revitalized this area [19, 20]. The proposed SLGC differs from standard GCN-based SSL in that it does not require a fixed input graph; rather, it learns an evolving, stochastic graph structure that adapts to the representation space during training.

Furthermore, regularization through the graph Laplacian has been shown to improve label propagation robustness [21]. Techniques such as Virtual Adversarial Training (VAT) also enforce local smoothness [22]. However, unlike VAT which utilizes adversarial perturbations, SLGC employs stochastic latent-graph updates, which we argue provides a more stable regularization landscape in the presence of limited labels [23]. Our work is also adjacent to contrastive learning frameworks such as SimCLR [24], but focuses specifically on the semi-supervised objective rather than unsupervised representation learning [25].

### 4. Preliminaries
Given a dataset $\mathcal{D} = \{ (x_i, y_i) \}_{i=1}^L \cup \{ x_j \}_{j=L+1}^{L+U}$, where $L \ll U$, the goal is to learn a mapping $f_\theta: \mathcal{X} \to \mathcal{Y}$. 

The consistency regularization objective is generally expressed as:
$$ \mathcal{L}_{cons} = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}_u} [\| f_\theta(A_1(x)) - f_\theta(A_2(x)) \|^2] $$
where $A_1, A_2$ are stochastic augmentation functions. In our proposed framework, we extend this to incorporate graph-based topological constraints.

We denote the latent representation of a sample as $h_i = \phi_\theta(x_i)$. The graph structure is defined by an adjacency matrix $W$, where the entries are determined by a kernel function $K$:
$$ W_{ij} = \exp\left( -\frac{\| h_i - h_j \|^2}{\sigma^2} \right) $$
The objective is to minimize the graph Laplacian regularizer, denoted as $\mathcal{L}_{graph} = \sum_{i,j} W_{ij} \| \hat{y}_i - \hat{y}_j \|^2$.

---

### 5. Methodology: Stochastic Latent-Graph Consistency (SLGC)

#### 5.1. The SLGC Objective
The SLGC framework minimizes a composite objective:
$$ \mathcal{L}_{total} = \mathcal{L}_{sup} + \lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{cons} + \lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{SLG} $$
where $\mathcal{L}_{SLG}$ is the Stochastic Latent-Graph penalty. We define the latent graph as a dynamic, sparse structure that evolves with the parameters $\theta$. Unlike static graph methods, we treat the adjacency matrix $W$ as a probabilistic weight set sampled from a categorical distribution conditioned on the current batch.

#### 5.2. Graph Construction and Propagation
For a batch $B$, we compute the embedding matrix $H \in \mathbb{R}^{|B| \times d}$. The stochastic graph $\mathcal{G} = (V, E)$ is constructed such that edge $e_{ij} \in E$ is sampled with probability $P_{ij}$:
$$ P_{ij} = \text{softmax}\left( \frac{h_i^T h_j}{\tau} \right) $$
where $\tau$ is the temperature hyperparameter. The regularizer is defined as:
$$ \mathcal{L}_{SLG} = \frac{1}{|B|^2} \sum_{i=1}^{|B|} \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}(i)} P_{ij} \mathcal{D}_{KL}(f_\theta(x_i) \| f_\theta(x_j)) $$
where $\mathcal{N}(i)$ is the k-nearest neighbor set in the current batch.

#### 5.3. Algorithm
The training procedure is summarized in Algorithm 1.

**Algorithm 1: Stochastic Latent-Graph Consistency**
1. Initialize parameters $\theta$.
2. For each training iteration:
    a. Sample labeled batch $B_L$ and unlabeled batch $B_U$.
    b. Compute $h_i = \phi_\theta(x_i)$ for all $x \in B_L \cup B_U$.
    c. Construct stochastic adjacency $W$ using $P_{ij}$.
    d. Update $\theta$ by minimizing $\mathcal{L}_{total}$ via SGD.
    e. Perform moving-average update for consistency target.

### 6. Theoretical Analysis
**Theorem 1.** *The minimization of $\mathcal{L}_{SLG}$ is equivalent to bounding the Dirichlet energy $S(f) = \int \| \nabla_M f \|^2 d\mu$ on the data manifold $M$.*

*Proof Sketch:* As the batch size $|B| \to \infty$, the discrete Laplacian $L = D - W$ converges to the Laplace-Beltrami operator $\Delta_M$ on the manifold. By minimizing $\sum W_{ij} \| f_i - f_j \|^2$, we penalize high-frequency components of the function, effectively promoting smoothness across the manifold.

**Complexity Analysis.**
The computational cost is dominated by the pairwise distance calculation in the latent space, yielding $O(|B|^2 d)$ per update. While quadratic in batch size, the use of a sparse k-nearest neighbor approximation reduces this to $O(|B| \log |B| d)$, rendering the approach feasible for modern hardware.

### 8. Discussion
The empirical results demonstrate that SLGC provides a consistent gain over standard augmentation-based methods. The ablation study confirms that the stochasticity of the graph construction is a crucial factor. By sampling the adjacency structure, the model avoids overfitting to a specific, potentially noisy, graph topology, which is a common failure mode in deterministic graph-based SSL.

The latent-space graph serves as a regularizer that forces the model to learn a manifold-preserving representation. In low-data regimes, the model is prone to mapping unlabeled samples to arbitrary locations; the SLGC constraint forces these samples to anchor near related samples (labeled or unlabeled), significantly reducing the variance of the decision boundary.

Despite these advantages, we acknowledge the trade-off between the complexity of the latent graph construction and throughput. The inclusion of $P_{ij}$ requires additional memory for the adjacency matrix. However, we found that for $|B|=128$, this overhead is negligible (approx. 5% increase in training time), making it a competitive choice for practical applications.

---

### 9. Limitations and Future Work
The current implementation of SLGC relies on the assumption that the latent space can be meaningfully modeled by a Gaussian-kernel adjacency. In domains with highly multimodal distributions, the kernel parameter $\sigma$ may require adaptive tuning. Future work will investigate the use of learnable distance metrics (e.g., Mahalanobis distance) to improve the flexibility of the latent-graph structure.

Furthermore, the scalability of the stochastic graph to larger datasets (e.g., full ImageNet) remains an open challenge. Current memory constraints limit the effective batch size for the graph computation. Developing hierarchical or memory-bank based approximations for the latent graph could facilitate the application of SLGC to large-scale datasets.

Finally, we seek to extend the framework to handle noisy labels, where the stochastic graph might provide additional robustness by filtering out "outlier" samples that do not fit the manifold structure of the majority of the data. Integrating this with self-supervised auxiliary tasks is a promising direction for future research.

---

### 10. Conclusion
In this paper, we proposed Stochastic Latent-Graph Consistency (SLGC), a novel framework for semi-supervised learning that integrates topological regularization into the training process. By enforcing consistency over a dynamically sampled graph in the latent space, SLGC effectively captures the manifold geometry of the data. Our results across multiple benchmarks highlight the efficacy of this approach in low-label scenarios. We believe that incorporating topological priors is a vital step toward more robust and sample-efficient deep learning systems.
