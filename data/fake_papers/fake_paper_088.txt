### Abstract
The paradigm of Federated Learning (FL) has emerged as a cornerstone for decentralized model training, yet it remains vulnerable to inference attacks that expose private local gradients. While Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC) provides cryptographic privacy, its computational overhead often renders large-scale deployment prohibitive. In this work, we propose **AdaSec-MPC**, a novel framework that integrates adaptive differential privacy (DP) with a lightweight, threshold-based SMPC protocol. By dynamically scaling the precision of secret-shared gradients based on the local loss landscape, AdaSec-MPC minimizes communication rounds while maintaining rigorous security guarantees. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that our approach achieves an $\epsilon$-differential privacy bound with a reduced communication complexity of $O(n \log d)$, where $n$ is the number of participants and $d$ the model dimension. Experiments on GLUE and CIFAR-10 benchmarks indicate that AdaSec-MPC maintains competitive utility compared to non-private baselines while outperforming standard SMPC-based aggregation by 42% in training throughput.

### Introduction
The proliferation of edge devices has necessitated the development of distributed machine learning paradigms that respect user data sovereignty. Federated Learning (FL) enables collaborative training by exchanging model updates rather than raw data [1]. However, the reconstruction of local inputs from shared gradients remains a persistent threat, with prior studies demonstrating that gradients can leak sensitive information about the training distribution [2].

To mitigate these risks, researchers have turned to Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC) and Differential Privacy (DP). SMPC allows for the summation of gradients without revealing individual contributions, but introduces significant latency [3]. Conversely, DP adds noise to gradients to provide mathematical privacy, often at the cost of model convergence speed and accuracy [4]. The central challenge is achieving a Pareto-optimal trade-off between privacy, communication efficiency, and global utility.

This paper introduces AdaSec-MPC, a framework that utilizes adaptive noise injection coupled with a truncated secret-sharing protocol. Unlike static SMPC implementations, AdaSec-MPC modulates the privacy budget $\epsilon$ dynamically, allowing for higher precision during early stages of convergence and increased obfuscation as the model approaches an optima. We provide a rigorous security analysis and empirical validation showing that AdaSec-MPC maintains utility while achieving orders of magnitude faster convergence compared to existing cryptographic primitives.

### Related Work
The literature on secure distributed learning is bifurcated into cryptographic approaches and statistical privacy frameworks. SMPC-based aggregation schemes, such as those proposed in [5] and [6], utilize secret-sharing to mask local updates. However, these methods are often limited by the quadratic communication complexity of multi-round protocols.

Differential privacy, popularized by [7], provides a robust mechanism for controlling privacy leakage. Extensions to the FL setting [8] have focused on local DP, which requires substantial noise injection to mask individual gradients, leading to significant degradation in model performance. Hybrid models that combine SMPC and DP exist [9], but current implementations fail to optimize the communication-privacy trade-off dynamically. Recent advancements in quantization techniques [10] and sparse communication [11] attempt to address efficiency, yet they often lack formal privacy guarantees in non-honest-but-curious threat models.

### Background and Preliminaries
Let $\mathcal{D} = \{D_1, D_2, \dots, D_n\}$ denote local datasets held by $n$ clients. The goal is to minimize the global objective function $F(w) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n f_i(w)$, where $f_i$ is the empirical risk on $D_i$.

**Definition 1 (Differential Privacy).** A randomized mechanism $\mathcal{M}$ is $(\epsilon, \delta)$-differentially private if for any two neighboring datasets $D$ and $D'$, $\Pr[\mathcal{M}(D) \in S] \leq e^\epsilon \Pr[\mathcal{M}(D') \in S] + \delta$.

**Definition 2 (Additive Secret Sharing).** To share a value $x \in \mathbb{Z}_q$ among $n$ parties, $x$ is partitioned into shares $x_i$ such that $\sum_{i=1}^n x_i \equiv x \pmod q$. No set of fewer than $n$ parties can reconstruct $x$ without the complete set of shares [12].

### Methodology: The AdaSec-MPC Framework
The AdaSec-MPC framework operates by interleaving local adaptive noise injection with a compressed secret-sharing protocol. The core innovation is the **Dynamic Precision Aggregator (DPA)**, which adjusts the quantization levels for secret sharing based on the gradient variance $\sigma^2_t$ at iteration $t$.

#### The AdaSec-MPC Protocol
1. **Local Noise Injection:** Each client $i$ computes a gradient $\nabla f_i(w_t)$ and adds Gaussian noise $\eta \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2_t I)$ to satisfy local $(\epsilon_t, \delta_t)$-DP.
2. **Dynamic Quantization:** The noisy gradient is projected into a lower-dimensional space using a learned mapping $\phi_t: \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{Z}_q^k$, where $k \ll d$.
3. **Secret Sharing:** The quantized vector is shared across $m$ nodes in the MPC cluster using Shamir's Secret Sharing [13].
4. **Secure Aggregation:** Nodes perform an additive homomorphic aggregation:
   $$ \text{Agg} = \sum_{i=1}^n \left( \phi_t(\nabla f_i(w_t) + \eta_i) \right) \pmod q $$
5. **Reconstruction:** The server recovers the aggregate and updates the global model $w_{t+1} = w_t - \gamma_t \text{Agg}^{-1}$.

#### Theoretical Analysis
We claim that AdaSec-MPC satisfies $(\epsilon, \delta)$-privacy by composition of the local noise and the threshold security of the secret sharing. Let the total privacy loss over $T$ iterations be bounded by:
$$ \sum_{t=1}^T \epsilon_t \leq \sqrt{2T \ln(1/\delta)} \epsilon + T \epsilon^2 $$
where $\epsilon_t$ is the budget per iteration. The communication cost is reduced via the projection $\phi_t$, which achieves an $O(n \log d)$ overhead, providing a significant improvement over the standard $O(n \cdot d)$ complexity [14].

### Algorithm 1: AdaSec-MPC Aggregation
```python
# Pseudocode for one global iteration
Input: Local gradients {g_1, ..., g_n}, privacy budget epsilon
Output: Global update delta_w

For each client i in parallel:
    sigma = compute_adaptive_noise(g_i, epsilon)
    g_noisy = g_i + N(0, sigma^2)
    g_quant = quantize(g_noisy, precision=p_t)
    shares = shamir_split(g_quant, threshold=t)
    send_shares_to_nodes(shares)

For each node in the MPC cluster:
    partial_sum = sum_received_shares()
    broadcast(partial_sum)

delta_w = reconstruct(partial_sum)
```

### Discussion
The empirical results demonstrate that AdaSec-MPC effectively bridges the gap between secure, private training and computational feasibility. The primary insight derived from the ablation study is the critical role of adaptive quantization; by reducing bits during the latter stages of training, the framework significantly lowers bandwidth requirements without sacrificing model convergence.

Furthermore, the privacy-utility trade-off is maintained at a level comparable to non-private baselines, suggesting that the overhead of cryptographic primitives is effectively mitigated. The integration of adaptive noise ensures that privacy budgets are not exhausted prematurely, allowing for a more granular control over the learning process.

Future work will focus on the extension of AdaSec-MPC to non-IID data distributions, which currently present a challenge for threshold-based secret sharing. Additionally, integrating hardware-based TEEs (Trusted Execution Environments) could further enhance the security guarantees of the MPC nodes.

### Limitations and Future Work
Despite its efficiency, AdaSec-MPC faces limitations in highly heterogeneous environments where local gradient variance is extreme. The current projection mechanism $\phi_t$ assumes a relatively stable latent space, which may not hold for non-stationary data streams.

Future research will explore the use of online gradient variance estimation to refine the $\phi_t$ projection parameters. Additionally, investigating the resilience of AdaSec-MPC against Byzantine adversaries remains a priority, as current security proofs assume an honest-but-curious model.

Finally, we intend to evaluate the framework on larger model architectures, such as Transformer-based LLMs, to determine the scalability of the SMPC aggregation protocol in high-dimensional settings.

### Conclusion
AdaSec-MPC provides a robust, efficient architecture for private distributed learning. By synthesizing adaptive DP and optimized SMPC, the framework offers a viable path toward privacy-compliant model development. Through theoretical and empirical rigor, this work highlights the potential for cryptographic and statistical privacy methods to coalesce into a single, high-performance training pipeline.
