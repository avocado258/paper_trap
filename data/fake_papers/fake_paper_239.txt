### 1. Abstract
Knowledge Graph Completion (KGC) remains a fundamental challenge in representation learning, particularly when capturing the hierarchical nature of relational data. While Euclidean embedding spaces are standard, they struggle to model tree-like structures without exponential distortion. In this paper, we propose **H-RelMan** (Hyperbolic Relational Manifolds), a novel framework that embeds entities and relations into a product manifold of varying constant negative curvature. By utilizing a curvature-adaptive Riemannian optimization strategy, our approach effectively maps hierarchical relationships into hyperbolic space, preserving structural properties that Euclidean models fail to represent. We demonstrate that H-RelMan achieves state-of-the-art performance on link prediction tasks across standard benchmarks (FB15k-237, WN18RR, YAGO3-10). Experimental results indicate significant improvements in mean reciprocal rank (MRR) and Hits@10 metrics, providing a robust theoretical foundation for modeling complex, multi-relational graph topologies.

Knowledge Graph Completion, Hyperbolic Geometry, Riemannian Manifolds, Relational Learning, Representation Learning.

### 3. Introduction
Knowledge Graphs (KGs) encode vast quantities of structured information, yet their utility is often constrained by incompleteness. Knowledge Graph Completion (KGC) seeks to infer missing facts, typically expressed as triplets $(h, r, t)$ representing head entity, relation, and tail entity. Standard approaches, such as TransE [1] and RotatE [2], represent these entities in Euclidean vector spaces. However, recent empirical evidence suggests that KGs exhibit latent hierarchical structures, which Euclidean embeddings struggle to represent without significant geometric distortion [3].

The embedding of hierarchical data in Euclidean space necessitates a dimensionality that grows linearly with the number of nodes to maintain low distortion [4]. Conversely, hyperbolic geometry—specifically the Poincaré ball model—provides a natural space for representing trees and hierarchies due to its exponential volume expansion [5]. While recent works have explored hyperbolic embeddings for simple graphs [6], they often overlook the complexity of multi-relational settings where relations themselves may possess hierarchical dependencies.

In this work, we introduce H-RelMan, a framework that projects entities onto a product space of hyperbolic manifolds with relation-specific curvature parameters. We hypothesize that by allowing curvature to be learned as a function of the relation, the model can dynamically adjust to the specific geometric requirements of different relational types. Our contributions are summarized as follows: (1) A novel Riemannian optimization objective for product hyperbolic manifolds; (2) A curvature-adaptive mechanism that assigns negative curvature values to relations; and (3) Extensive experimental validation demonstrating superior performance over existing Euclidean and fixed-curvature hyperbolic baselines.

### 4. Related Work
Knowledge graph embedding methods are traditionally categorized into translational models and semantic matching models. TransE [1] initiated the translational paradigm, mapping relations to vectors in $\mathbb{R}^d$. This was subsequently extended by RotatE [2], which models relations as rotations in complex space to capture symmetry and composition. Semantic matching models, such as DistMult [7] and ComplEx [8], utilize bilinear forms to score triplets, though they often lack the capacity to capture hierarchical properties.

Hyperbolic embeddings have recently gained traction in NLP and graph learning. Nickel and Kiela [9] demonstrated that Poincaré embeddings effectively capture hierarchy in taxonomic data. Building on this, Ganea et al. [10] extended hyperbolic embeddings to neural networks, facilitating the construction of hyperbolic graph convolutional networks. However, these methods typically assume uniform curvature, which is insufficient for heterogeneous KGs containing both hierarchical (e.g., "is-a") and non-hierarchical (e.g., "born-in") relations.

Recent advancements in Riemannian geometry for machine learning have enabled optimization on manifolds [11]. Chen et al. [12] proposed hyperbolic relational models, but their approach remains limited by static curvature assumptions. Our work deviates from these by introducing a learnable curvature parameter $\kappa_r$ for each relation, allowing the manifold's geometry to adapt to the relational semantics.

### 5. Background / Preliminaries
#### 5.1 Poincaré Ball Model
The Poincaré ball model $(\mathbb{B}^d_c, g_c)$ with constant negative curvature $c > 0$ is defined by the manifold $\mathbb{B}^d_c = \{x \in \mathbb{R}^d : \|x\| < 1/\sqrt{c}\}$. The Riemannian metric tensor is given by:
$$g_x^c = \lambda_x^c{}^2 g^E, \quad \text{where } \lambda_x^c = \frac{2}{1 - c\|x\|^2}$$
where $g^E$ is the Euclidean metric. The distance between two points $u, v \in \mathbb{B}^d_c$ is:
$$d_c(u, v) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{c}} \text{artanh}(\sqrt{c} \| -u \oplus_c v \|)$$
where $\oplus_c$ denotes the Möbius addition [13].

#### 5.2 Möbius Transformations
To facilitate translation and rotation in hyperbolic space, we utilize the Möbius gyrovector space. For $u, v \in \mathbb{B}^d_c$, addition is defined as:
$$u \oplus_c v = \frac{(1 + 2c\langle u, v \rangle + c\|v\|^2)u + (1 - c\|u\|^2)v}{1 + 2c\langle u, v \rangle + c^2\|u\|^2\|v\|^2}$$
This allows for the composition of relational transformations within the hyperbolic manifold, preserving the underlying curvature $c$.

### 6. Methodology / Proposed Approach
#### 6.1 The H-RelMan Framework
We propose a model where each entity $e \in \mathcal{E}$ is an embedding $x_e \in \mathbb{B}^d_{\kappa_r}$. Each relation $r \in \mathcal{R}$ is characterized by a curvature $\kappa_r > 0$ and a Möbius transformation matrix $M_r$. The scoring function is defined as the hyperbolic distance between the transformed head entity and the tail entity:
$$f(h, r, t) = -d_{\kappa_r}(M_r \otimes_{\kappa_r} x_h, x_t)^2$$
where $\otimes_{\kappa_r}$ denotes the Möbius scalar-matrix multiplication.

#### 6.2 Curvature-Adaptive Optimization
To optimize $\kappa_r$, we define the gradient flow on the product manifold $\mathcal{M} = \prod_{r \in \mathcal{R}} \mathbb{B}^d_{\kappa_r}$. Given a triplet loss $\mathcal{L}$, the update rule for curvature $\kappa_r$ is:
$$\kappa_r \leftarrow \text{Proj}_{\mathbb{R}^+} (\kappa_r - \eta \nabla_{\kappa_r} \mathcal{L})$$
where $\nabla_{\kappa_r} \mathcal{L}$ is the Riemannian gradient with respect to the curvature parameter. This allows the model to "flatten" relations that do not exhibit hierarchical structure while "curving" those that do.

#### 6.3 Algorithm
The training procedure is summarized in Algorithm 1.

**Algorithm 1: H-RelMan Training**
1. Initialize $x_e \in \mathbb{B}^d_{\kappa_0}, M_r \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}, \kappa_r \in \mathbb{R}^+$.
2. **For** each epoch:
3.   Sample batch of triplets $\mathcal{B} = \{(h, r, t)\}$.
4.   Compute embeddings $x_h, x_t$ in $\mathbb{B}^d_{\kappa_r}$.
5.   Compute score $f(h, r, t)$ using Eq (1).
6.   Compute loss $\mathcal{L} = \sum \max(0, \gamma - f(h, r, t) + f(h', r, t'))$.
7.   Update $x_e, M_r$ via Riemannian SGD [14].
8.   Update $\kappa_r$ via gradient descent.
9. **End For**

### 7. Theoretical Analysis
**Theorem 1.** *The distortion of a tree with branching factor $b$ embedded in a hyperbolic space of curvature $\kappa$ is bounded by $O(\frac{1}{\sqrt{\kappa}} \log b)$.* 
*Proof Sketch:* By mapping the tree nodes to the Poincaré disk, the volume growth of the hyperbolic space compensates for the exponential growth of the tree, resulting in zero asymptotic distortion as $\kappa \to 1$ [15].

**Complexity Analysis.** The computational complexity per triplet update is $O(d^2)$, where $d$ is the embedding dimension, due to the Möbius matrix multiplication. This is comparable to Euclidean translational models, ensuring scalability to large KGs.

### 8. Experiments
#### 8.1 Datasets
We evaluate on three standard datasets:
1. **WN18RR:** A subset of WordNet, highly hierarchical.
2. **FB15k-237:** A subset of Freebase, containing complex relational patterns.
3. **YAGO3-10:** A large-scale KG with diverse facts.

#### 8.2 Experimental Setup
We use Adam optimizer with learning rate 0.001. Embedding dimension $d$ is set to 200. Batch size is 512. Negative sampling ratio is 1:10.

### 9. Discussion / Insights
The experimental results confirm that H-RelMan consistently outperforms Euclidean baselines, particularly on hierarchical datasets like WN18RR. The significant gain observed in the ablation study upon removing the variable curvature parameter suggests that the geometric flexibility provided by $\kappa_r$ is critical for modeling heterogeneous relations.

Furthermore, we observe that the model effectively learns to assign higher curvature values to relations that represent taxonomic hierarchies (e.g., "hypernym"), while lower values are assigned to symmetric or associative relations. This dynamic allocation of geometric space provides a novel lens for analyzing the latent structure of knowledge graphs.

Despite these gains, the computational overhead of Riemannian optimization remains non-negligible compared to simple Euclidean updates. Future work will investigate the use of stochastic manifold approximations to further reduce training time while maintaining geometric integrity.

---

### 10. Limitations & Future Work
A primary limitation of H-RelMan is the constraint on the number of relations. As the number of relations grows in large-scale graphs (e.g., Wikidata), the parameter space for $\{\kappa_r\}_{r \in \mathcal{R}}$ may become over-parameterized. Implementing a hierarchical prior on $\kappa_r$ could mitigate this issue.

Additionally, the current model assumes a constant curvature within each relation. Extending this to allow curvature to vary across the manifold based on local entity density could lead to further improvements. We plan to investigate dynamic, entity-dependent curvature manifolds in future research.

Finally, the integration of H-RelMan with graph neural networks (GNNs) remains an open challenge. Combining hyperbolic relational embeddings with message-passing architectures could provide a more robust approach to multi-hop reasoning in KGs.

---

### 11. Conclusion
We have presented H-RelMan, a novel KGC framework utilizing product hyperbolic manifolds with relation-specific curvature. By mapping entities to a space that naturally reflects hierarchical data, our model achieves state-of-the-art performance on link prediction tasks. The framework provides a rigorous geometric foundation for future research into relational representation learning.
